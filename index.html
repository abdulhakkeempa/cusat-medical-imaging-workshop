About

Read more about our workshop

Medical imaging has been one of the most remarkable developments in clinical medicine over the past millennium. A longer life expectancy is directly related to it, altering how doctors think, manage, and treat medical disorders. Artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning are the next medical imaging paradigm to advance the knowledge frontiers and improve people's lives through cutting-edge research in artificial intelligence and computer-aided image analysis. By exploiting artificial intelligence techniques, cognitive computing power can be imparted to computers enabling them to be proficient in recognizing images. With the advancements in imaging and scanning techniques, a surge has been witnessed in the development of more innovative applications based on AI and Computer Vision. Deep Learning and Artificial Neural Networks are currently being adopted to address a wide range of challenges in the field of Medical Image Understanding. In this School, practitioners, researchers, and academicians will join hands to discuss recent theoretical and practical aspects of 'Medical Images understanding'. Experts from National & International Organizations will lead the technical sessions to form a network of researchers to collaborate and advance the research and development in the field.

Cochin University of Science and Technology

The Department of Computer Science came into existence in 1984 with an M.Tech. course in Computer and Information Science, which was the first in the state of Kerala. In 1986, the Department was identified by the Defence Research and Development Organisation for conducting an M.Sc. programme in Computer Software, as a feeder course to the DRDO Laboratories. The department served as a seeding agent for the establishment of Computer Centre, which was inaugurated by the then Prime Minister in 1990,.The department was entrusted with the responsibility of conducting the MCA programme in June 1994, which helped to streamline the course and setting it into the right track. Currently the department runs MTech programmes in various specialisations, Five year Integrated Msc course and PhD. Active research works happen in the areas of Artificial Intelligence, Computer Vision, Networking, Information Systems Engg, etc. Till date, the department has produced around 40 PhDs.
